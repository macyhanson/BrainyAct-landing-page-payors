What BrainyAct Is

Whole child. Multi-condition care.

BrainyAct supports individuals ages 6+ with learning, attention, and neurodevelopmental challenges, with or without a formal diagnosis, including autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, sensory processing disorders, dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, anxiety, and related needs.

It is not another passive app. It is an active, motion-based therapeutic program that uses the body to train the brain. Participants move, respond, balance, coordinate, track, process, and engage through exercises designed to strengthen the foundational systems that support daily function.

What BrainyAct Measures

Progress across six functional domains.

The purpose is not to generate more utilization. It is to show whether the child or young adult is building the foundational skills needed to function with more confidence, regulation, and independence.

How progress is measured
Baseline
Structured baseline
Each participant is assessed across developmental and functional domains before training begins.
Ongoing
Repeated in-platform assessment
The platform re-measures the same skill areas throughout the program window, not only at the end.
Review
Functional domain review
Progress is reviewed across the six domains and shared with families and care teams as structured data.

BrainyAct is not replacing care.
It gives the system a better starting point.

Many children are placed into years of services before foundational brain-body gaps have been addressed. BrainyAct starts earlier in the chain, and when those systems improve, learning and behavior can become easier to support.
